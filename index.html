About

My training centers on extracting biological insight from complex imaging and spatial molecular data. During my undergraduate research at UCLA in Dr. Hong Zhou's lab, I applied cryo-electron tomography to characterize microbial and viral architecture in near-native states and contributed to scalable computational workflows for automated particle picking.

At Cedars-Sinai in Dr. Simon Knott's lab, I develop interpretable spatial analysis strategies to quantify how tumor, immune, and stromal cells are organized at diagnosis and remodeled after neoadjuvant therapy, with and without anti-PD-1 immunotherapy. My long-term goal is to become a hybrid wet-lab and computational scientist who bridges mechanism and translational impact in breast cancer research.

Research Focus

  • Build interpretable predictors of neoadjuvant therapy response in triple-negative breast cancer using single-cell spatial transcriptomics.
  • Characterize tumor-immune boundaries, stromal architecture, and spatial neighborhoods associated with pathologic complete response or high-risk residual disease.
  • Prioritize resistance mechanisms in the tumor microenvironment for cross-modality and functional validation in tractable systems.
  • Integrate computational spatial analysis with wet-lab immunology and murine model training to accelerate biomarker and target discovery.
